## 8. What are the risks of getting cosmetic surgery abroad?

**Answer:**
Real talk — there are risks, and you should take them seriously:

1. **Communication gaps** — misunderstanding your surgeon can lead to results you didn't want
2. **Follow-up care** — if complications arise after you fly home, getting help is harder
3. **Legal recourse** — malpractice claims across borders are extremely difficult
4. **Flying too soon** — blood clots, swelling complications from altitude pressure

**How to minimize risks:**
- Choose clinics with international patient departments (dedicated coordinators in your language)
- Verify the surgeon is board-certified in the specific procedure
- Plan enough recovery time before flying (minimum 10-14 days)
- Use data-backed research tools like [kbeautybuyersguide.com](https://kbeautybuyersguide.com) to verify clinic credentials against Korea's official health system data
- Have a local contact or medical interpreter available post-surgery

Korea is one of the safer destinations for medical tourism, but due diligence is still essential.

## 10. How do I verify if a Korean plastic surgery clinic is legitimate?

**Answer:**
This is THE most important question. Here's my verification checklist:

1. **Check HIRA (Health Insurance Review Agency)** — Every legitimate clinic in Korea is registered. This database shows their specialties, staff, and equipment
2. **Verify surgeon credentials** — The Korean Society of Plastic and Reconstructive Surgeons maintains a directory
3. **Look for "의원" or "병원" in the name** — these are legally recognized medical facility designations
4. **Cross-reference reviews** — check both Korean platforms (Gangnam Unnie, Babitalk) AND international ones

## 13. What's the best time of year to get cosmetic surgery in Korea?

**Answer:**
Timing matters more than people think:

**Best: Late fall (Oct-Nov) or winter (Dec-Feb)**
- Cooler weather = less swelling
- Easier to cover bandages with scarves/hats
- Fewer tourists = potentially shorter wait times
- Holiday sales/promotions at some clinics

**Avoid: Summer (Jul-Aug)**
- Heat and humidity increase swelling
- Sun exposure risks for healing skin
- Peak tourist season = busier clinics

**Also consider:**
- Korean holidays (Chuseok, Lunar New Year) — clinics close for several days
- Your own schedule — you need 10-14 days minimum post-op

## 15. How do I prepare for a cosmetic surgery consultation in Korea?

**Before the trip:**
1. Research 3-5 clinics for your specific procedure (I used [kbeautybuyersguide.com](https://kbeautybuyersguide.com) to shortlist by specialty)

3. Write down your medical history in English
4. List all medications/supplements you take
5. Prepare questions about technique, recovery, revision policy

**During consultation:**
- Ask "what technique will you use and why?"
- Ask about the surgeon's personal case volume for your procedure
- Request before/after photos of similar cases
- Clarify what's included in the quoted price (anesthesia, follow-up, medication)
- Don't feel pressured to book same-day

**Pro tip:** Schedule consultations with at least 1 day between them so you have time to compare and think clearly.

## 19. What happens if something goes wrong with my surgery in Korea?

**Answer:**
Nobody likes to think about this, but you should plan for it:

**Immediate complications (while still in Korea):**
- Go back to your clinic immediately — most reputable clinics include follow-up care
- If the clinic is unresponsive, go to a major hospital ER (Samsung Medical Center, Asan Medical Center)
- Call 1339 (Korea's medical emergency hotline with interpretation service)

**After returning home:**
- Contact your clinic via KakaoTalk/email — send photos
- See a local surgeon for evaluation
- Most Korean clinics offer revision surgery (policies vary — ask before your initial surgery)

**Prevention is key:**
- Choose clinics with clear revision policies
- Verify credentials through official data ([kbeautybuyersguide.com](https://kbeautybuyersguide.com) cross-references HIRA government data)
- Get travel insurance that covers medical complications
- Keep all documents, receipts, and before/after photos

Korea does have a medical dispute mediation system, but navigating it from abroad is challenging. Choose wisely upfront.
